摘 要 
这篇论文标志着在基于深度学习的图像分类领域，特别是针对埃塞俄比亚传统药用
植物的创新努力。这项研究以其致力于解决埃塞俄比亚传统医学中的独特挑战而著称，
包括意识空白、文献不足和生物多样性保护，突显了埃塞俄比亚传统医学在医疗保健方
面的重要意义。 这项研究的创新之处在于开发了一个包含24,000 张图像的全面数据集，
代表了 64 种埃塞俄比亚传统药用植物的植物物种，填补了现有文献中的重要空白，并
确保了专门针对埃塞俄比亚背景设计的分类系统的准确性和相关性。 
在方法上，该研究采用了多方面的方法，包括自定义卷积神经网络（CNN）架构和使
用视觉几何组（VGG16）、Inception版本 3（InceptionV3）、Inception 残差网络版本2
（InceptionResNetV2）和Mobile 网络版本2（MobileNetV2）等已建立的预训练模型进
行迁移学习。 训练多个模型并进行比较分析突显了系统方法和确定任务中最有效模型的
目标。通过超参数调整和严格评估，InceptionResNetV2 以出色的 97.6％准确率脱颖而
出。 
在模型开发之外，该研究通过创建一个用户友好的移动和Web 应用程序界面来扩展
其影响，以部署模型，探索量化以提高模型效率。这使用户可以轻松识别植物并获取全
面的药用信息。该应用程序的离线功能设计为在网络连接有限的偏远地区使用。 
这些发现代表了在基于深度学习的图像分类方面的显着进展，对埃塞俄比亚传统医
学具有深远的影响。 通过解决关键挑战并强调实际应用性， 这项研究不仅使科学界受益，
还为传统医学从业者带来了希望，并为这一崇高领域的进一步探索奠定了坚实基础。 
 
关键词：深度学习、传统医学、CNN、迁移学习、图像预处理、信息检索系统

Abstract 
This thesis marks a n innovative effort in the domain of deep learning -based image 
classification, specifically tailored to Ethiopian traditional medicinal plants. Distinguished by 
its commitment to addressing unique challenges within Ethiopian traditional medicine, 
including awareness gaps , inadequate documentation,  and biodiversity preservation, this 
research highlights the profound significance of Ethiopian traditional medicine in healthcare 
provision. Center to the novelty of this research is the development of a comprehensive dataset 
comprising 24,000 images representing 64 plant species of Ethiopian traditional medicinal 
plants stands as a testament to this commitment, filling a crucial void in existing literature and 
ensuring the accuracy and relevance of the classification system specifically designed for the 
Ethiopian context. 
Methodologically, the study adopts a multifaceted approach, encompassing custom 
Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) architecture and transfer learning with well-established 
pre-trained models including Visual Geometry Group (VGG16), Inception Version 3 
(InceptionV3), Inception Residual Network Version 2 (InceptionResNetV2), and Mobile 
Network Version 2 (MobileNetV2).  Training multiple models and conducting comparative 
analysis underscores the systematic method and the goal of identifying the most effective 
model for the task at hand.  Through hyperparameter tuning and rigorous evaluation , 
InceptionResNetV2 emerges as a standout performer with exceptional 97.6% accuracy.  
Beyond model development, the research extends its impact through the creation of a user-
friendly mobile and web application interface  for model deployment after exploration of 
quantization to enhance model efficiency. This  empowers users to seamlessly identify plants 
and access comprehensive medicinal information. Designed for accessibility, the application's 
offline functionality ensures usability even in remote areas with limited internet connectivity. 
These findings represent a notable advance in deep learning -based image classification, 
with profound implications for Ethiopian traditional medicine. By addressing critical 
challenges and emphasizing practical applicability, this research not only benefits the scientific 
community but also holds p romise for traditional medicine practitioners, setting a firm 
foundation for further exploration in this noble area of study. 
Keywords: Deep learning, Traditional medicine, CNN, Transfer learning, Image 
preprocessing, Information retrieval system

1 Introduction 
1.1 Background 
In recent years, significant advancements in deep learning, particularly within the realm of 
image classification, have underscored its efficacy. As a subset of machine learning, deep 
learning excels in image classification tasks by learning complex hierarchical representa tions 
directly from raw data [1,2]. CNNs, a prominent type of deep learning model, have shown 
outstanding performance across various applications including image classification, object 
detection, and image segmentation[3–5]. Particularly in plant classification, CNNs have proven 
highly effective, distinguishing plant species with remarkable accuracy based on distinct leaf 
features like patterns, shapes, textures, and veins [6–8]. This capability to autonomously extract 
and analyze image features enables precise species identification and categorization. 
Ethiopia, often referred to as the “cradle of mankind”, is known for its diverse landscapes, 
climates, and natural resources, which support a variety of flora and fauna [9]. It has been 
recognized as one of the most biodiverse countries in Africa, with approximately 6,000 plant 
species, 10% of which are endemic to Ethiopia [10]. Among these plant species, about 800 are 
utilized for medicinal purposes by various ethnic groups and local healers [11,12]. Ethiopian 
traditional medicinal plants have long been used as a fundamental part of healthcare practices, 
with a rich heritage of botanical knowledge passed down through generations. These plants are 
employed in the treatment of a wide range of health problems including malaria, diarrhea, 
wounds, infections, diabetes, hypertension, and more. Studies have shown that the main reason 
for relying on traditional medicine is the lack of access to modern healthcare options[13–15].
